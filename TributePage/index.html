Doctor Nianshuang Wang

Dr. Nianshuang Wang, a distinguished scientist and a pioneer in the field of Structural Biology and Virology, has made significant contributions that have left an indelible mark on the scientific community.

Early Life and Education

Dr. Wang embarked on his scientific journey at Tsinghua University, where he earned his PhD degree in 2014.

Career and Contributions

Dr. Wang currently serves as a Research Fellow at the University of Texas and a scintist at Regeneron Pharmaceuticals. His work has been cited over 18,446 times since 2019, reflecting the impact and reach of his research.

Groundbreaking Research

During his PhD, he determined the structural mechanism of MERS-CoV receptor recognition. In partnership with Linqi Zhang's lab in 2014, they isolated the first monoclonal neutralizing antibody against MERS-CoV.

In 2016, he developed a universal method to lock the shapeshifting S protein in the "prefusion" state by introducing two prolines into the protein. This technology, known as S-2P, was successfully applied in at least five major COVID-19 vaccines.

Dr. Wang's research has been in numerous high-impact journals. Some of his most cited works include:

  • Cryo-EM structure of the 2019-nCoV spike in the prefusion conformation
  • SARS-CoV-2 mRNA vaccine design
  • Immunogenecity and structures of a rationally designed prefusion MERS-CoV spike antigen
  • Structure-based design of prefusion-stabilized SARS-CoV-2 spikes
  • Structure of MERS-CoV spike receptor-binding domain complexed with human receptor DPP4
  • Pre-fusion structure of a human coronavirus spike protein
  • Immunogenecity of a DNA vaccine candidate for COVID-19
